Explanation of my dataset and proof that it is viable¶

  • HomeTeam: Team playing at their home ground. They face the team playing away.
  • AwayTeam: Team playing away from their home ground. They face the team playing at home.
  • FTHG: Full Time Home Goals. Number of goals home team scored at the end of the match.
  • FTAG: Full Time Away Goals. Number of goals away team scored at the end of the match.
  • FTR: Full Time Result. H: Home team won, D: Draw, A: Away team won
  • HTHG: Half Time Home Goals. Number of goals home team scored until half time.
  • HTAG: Half Time Away Goals. Number of goals away team scored until half time.
  • HS: Home Shots. Number of shots home team had in the whole match.
  • AS: Away Shots. Number of shots away team had in the whole match.
  • HST: Home Shots on Target. Number of shots home team had on target (at the away teams goal) in the whole match.
  • AST: Away Shots on Target. Number of shots away team had on target (at the home teams goal) in the whole match.
  • HF: Home Fouls. Number of fouls home team committed in the whole match.
  • AF: Away Fouls. Number of fouls away team committed in the whole match.
  • HC: Home Corners. Number of corners home team had in the whole match.
  • AC: Away Corners. Number of corners away team had in the whole match.
  • HY: Home Yellow. Number of yellow cards home team received in the whole match.
  • AY: Away Yellow. Number of yellow cards away team received in the whole match.
  • HR: Home Red. Number of red cards home team received in the whole match.
  • AR: Away Red. Number of red cards away team received in the whole match.
  • B365H: Bet365Home. Odds of home team winning as per the betting company Bet365.
  • B365D: Bet365Draw. Odds of a draw as per the betting company Bet365.
  • B365A: Bet365Away. Odds of away team winning as per the betting company Bet365.

What is the structure of your dataset?¶

There are 3420 games in the dataset with 22 features, described above. Most variables are numeric in nature, but the variable FTR is a non-ordinal categorical value. The variables B365H, B365D, and B365A are the betting odds for that particular match given by the betting company Bet365. The variable with the lowest value, is the variable deemed most likely to occur. For example, consider the odds are B365H: 2.00, B365D: 3.30, B365A: 4.0. B365H has the lowest value of 2.00, therefore it is considered the most likely to occur, and Bet365 predicted the home team were most likely to win.

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

I'm interested in figuring out what features are best for predicting the FTR (Full Time Result) of a match.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

I expect that the number of shots each team has (HS, AS) as well as the number of shots on target each team has (HST, AST) will have a large factor in deciding the FTR. The number of corners may also have an effect in deciding the FTR, but to a smaller degree.

Running a simple correlation plot shows us which values are most highly correlated. As expected, FTHG and HTHG are highly correlated with each other, as are FTAG and HTAG. This is because the score at half time is likely to be either the same or slightly higher than the score at full time. If the number of half time goals increases, then the full time goals is also likely to increase.

We also see that HS and AS have a negative correlation with each other, which is to be expected, as if the home team has more shots, they likely have more possession, making it less likely for the away team to get the ball and then have a shot.

There is also a strong positive correlation between HS with HC, and AS with AC. This is likely due to the fact that a team that has a lot of shots is likely to also have a lot of corners, as those shots end up being deflected behind the goal for a corner.
